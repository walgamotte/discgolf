# The Core Release Angles
Every airborne shot relies on the initial angle of the disc relative to the ground. All descriptions below are from the perspective of a Right-Handed Backhand (RHBH) thrower.

* **Hyzer**: The outer edge of the disc is tilted downward, away from your body. It naturally curves sharply to the left.
* **Flat**: The disc is released perfectly parallel to the ground. 
* **Anhyzer**: The outer edge of the disc is tilted upward. It forces the disc to travel to the right.

---

# The 9 Basic Shot Shapes
These shapes are achieved by combining the three release angles with the three main disc stabilities: **Overstable (OS)**, **Stable/Neutral (S)**, and **Understable (US)**.

### 1. Pure Hyzer
* **The Shape**: A smooth, predictable arc curving continuously to the left.
* **Disc Used**: Overstable or Stable.
* **Execution**: Tilt your wrist downward at your reach-back. Follow through along that same upward, tilted angle.

### 2. Spike Hyzer
* **The Shape**: A very high, nearly vertical flight path that crashes straight down into the ground to limit skipping.
* **Disc Used**: Extremely Overstable.
* **Execution**: Lean your upper body forward over your toes. Throw the disc high into the air with an extreme hyzer tilt. 

### 3. Hyzer Flip
* **The Shape**: The disc is released on a hyzer, flips up to fly dead straight, and finishes with a gentle fade.
* **Disc Used**: Understable.
* **Execution**: Throw an understable disc with a moderate hyzer angle. Throw with high speed so the aerodynamic turn pulls the disc flat mid-air.

### 4. Straight Shot
* **The Shape**: A laser-straight flight from release to landing.
* **Disc Used**: Stable/Neutral.
* **Execution**: Pull the disc cleanly through the "power pocket" (across your chest). Commit to a perfectly flat plane and straight follow-through.

### 5. Turnover
* **The Shape**: Released flat or on a mild hyzer, the disc continuously drifts to the right and finishes right without fading back.
* **Disc Used**: Understable.
* **Execution**: Throw the disc flat and with high velocity. The disc's understability will pull it into a rightward glide.

### 6. Tail-Off Anhyzer
* **The Shape**: Starts by moving right, holds the line, and levels out or drops softly at the end.
* **Disc Used**: Understable or Neutral.
* **Execution**: Tilt your spine slightly backward at the waist. Release the disc on an anhyzer angle (outer edge up).

### 7. Flex Shot
* **The Shape**: An "S-curve" where the disc starts out traveling to the right, then fights back hard to finish on the left.
* **Disc Used**: Overstable.
* **Execution**: Force an overstable disc onto a steep anhyzer release angle. The disc will fight against the angle, creating a sweeping right-to-left arc.

### 8. S-Shot (Helix)
* **The Shape**: A wide, sweeping "S" flight designed for maximum distance.
* **Disc Used**: Understable.
* **Execution**: Release flat or with a tiny hyzer. The disc will naturally turn hard to the right before running out of speed and fading back left.

### 9. Cut Roller
* **The Shape**: The disc hits the ground on a sharp angle and rolls along the turf to navigate low ceilings or tight corners.
* **Disc Used**: Understable (for long distance) or Overstable (for sharp, short corners).
* **Execution**: Throw the disc on a very steep anhyzer angle, targeting a landing point 30-50 feet in front of you so it hits the ground rolling on its edge.

# Putting Styles
Putting requires prioritizing accuracy and control over raw speed and distance.

* **Push Putt**: The disc is released with minimal spin using a levering motion of the arm.
    * *Execution*: Keep your wrist stiff. Swing your arm vertically from your thigh toward the target like a pendulum, popping the disc out using your fingers.
* **Spin Putt**: A putt that relies on heavy wrist snap to generate spin and a straight trajectory.
    * *Execution*: Tuck the disc slightly into your stomach. Chop your wrist forward sharply to create high spin, making the putt more resilient to wind.
* **Jump / Step Putt**: Used for long-range putts outside the 10-meter circle to add leg power.
    * *Execution*: Launch your lower body forward as you throw. Your trailing foot must leave the ground (jump) or step forward after the disc is legally released.
* **Turbo Putt**: An overhand putting style used to see over high obstacles.
    * *Execution*: Hold the disc above your shoulder like a waiter holding a tray, with your thumb in the center underneath and fingers on the outer rim. Push your hand straight forward toward the basket.

# Scramble & Utility Shots
These emergency throws are critical for escaping thick brush or woods.

* **Tomahawk**: An overhand throw that flies vertically and mirrors a baseball pitching motion.
    * *Execution*: Use a forehand grip. Hold the disc completely vertical over your head and throw it forward. It will flip upside down and glide left-to-right.
* **Thumber**: Another overhand option that flips in the exact opposite direction of a tomahawk.
    * *Execution*: Wrap your thumb tightly inside the inner rim with your fingers on top of the flight plate. Throw overhand; the disc will pan out right-to-left.
* **Patent Pending**: A backward-facing stance used when you are pinned behind a massive obstacle.
    * *Execution*: Place your back heel directly on your lie. Put your other foot far out into the open fairway, look away from the target, and throw a blind backhand away from the obstruction.
* **Skip Shot**: A low-altitude throw designed to strike the ground hard and ricochet sharply around an obstacle.
    * *Execution*: Select a wide, flat-rimmed overstable driver in durable premium plastic. Throw it low to the ground on a sharp hyzer angle.
